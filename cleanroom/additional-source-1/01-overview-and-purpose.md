# Additional Source 1 — overview and purpose

## What AS1 represents

**Additional Source 1 (AS1)** documents **Netcracker** as described in **public marketing**, **product portfolio pages**, **blogs**, and **analyst solution profiles**—not as reverse-engineered software. Netcracker is a **commercial** supplier of **digital OSS and BSS** solutions, historically strong in **communications service providers (CSPs)** and large-scale telecom operations.

## Why include a non-open product

Open-source references (Source A and Sources B–H) reflect **downloadable** designs. AS1 illustrates how **enterprise telco vendors** frame **inventory, orchestration, and assurance** when selling to operators at scale—useful for **contrast** (product packaging, commercial integrations, “active inventory” narrative) even without source access.

## Stated corporate facts (public)

- Long-established vendor in telecom OSS/BSS; **headquartered in the United States** (Waltham, Massachusetts) with **global** delivery and a **large employee base** per public company descriptions.
- **Subsidiary** relationship with **NEC Corporation** is widely cited in public materials.
- **Portfolio breadth** spans BSS, OSS, customer experience, orchestration, and automation—inventory is one pillar inside a **much larger** commercial stack.

## Relationship to Source A

**Source A** (see `cleanroom/source-a/`) targets **network source of truth and automation** in a **single open product** with plugins. **AS1** targets **operator-scale OSS/BSS** where **inventory**, **service inventory**, and **orchestration** are marketed as **integrated** with **billing, catalog, assurance**, and **partner ecosystems**. The **buyer**, **deployment model**, and **integration surface** differ materially from a self-hosted NetBox-class tool.
