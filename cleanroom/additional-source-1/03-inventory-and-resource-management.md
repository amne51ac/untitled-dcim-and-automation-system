# Additional Source 1 — inventory and resource management (advertised)

## Active Resource Inventory

Public blogs and product messaging promote **Active Resource Inventory** as a **real-time** (or near-real-time) system that maintains visibility over **logical** and **physical** resources across **hybrid** environments. Advertised goals include:

- **Assigning, reassigning, monitoring**, and **managing** diverse resources **on demand**.
- Supporting both **current** operational state and **planned** states as part of a **single source of truth** narrative for downstream OSS/BSS consumers.
- Enabling **optimization** of resource use and identification of **faulty** or **at-risk** states when combined with assurance feeds.

## Consolidated / legacy naming

Industry and older collateral sometimes refer to **Consolidated Resource Inventory** or broad **Resource Inventory** as a **central repository** for **topology**, **configuration**, **status**, **connectivity**, and **assignment**—feeding **planning**, **fulfillment**, and **assurance**. Netcracker’s current public naming favors **Active** inventory in cloud-era messaging.

## Service Inventory

**Service Inventory+** (public product naming) is described as a **centralized** view of **provisioned services** spanning **traditional**, **hybrid**, and **virtual** infrastructures—linking **customer-facing services** to **resource** consumption.

## Physical and logical scope (historical public summaries)

Marketing and older public overviews list themes such as:

- **Physical and logical** resource modeling, **connectivity**, **equipment libraries**, and **location** hierarchies.
- **Number and address** management (including **IP** pools and **telecom numbering** contexts in carrier settings).
- **Asset** tracking (including **CPE**, **spares**, **warehouse**-style processes in some descriptions).
- **Visualization**: topology views, **floor/rack/shelf** style representations where offered in portfolio materials.

## Contrast with Source A

Source A’s inventory is **open**, **community-documented**, and **developer-extensible** with a clear **DCIM/IPAM** heritage. AS1 inventory is **commercial**, **CSP-oriented**, and **bundled** with **BSS** and **orchestration** as a **suite sale**—different **packaging** and **integration depth**, not a direct feature checklist match.
