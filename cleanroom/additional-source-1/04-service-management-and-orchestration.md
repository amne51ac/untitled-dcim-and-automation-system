# Additional Source 1 — service management and orchestration (advertised suite)

Public portfolio pages for **Service Management & Orchestration** (Digital OSS) describe a **componentized** suite. Names and capabilities below follow **vendor marketing**; they are **not** implementation specs.

## Advertised components (family-level)

| Public component name (examples) | Advertised role (summary) |
|----------------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Service Catalog+** | Manage **service templates** and **specifications** for repeatable design and delivery; partner and carrier service definitions. |
| **Service Inventory+** | End-to-end **service** view across infrastructures (see also topic 03). |
| **Service Orchestration+** | **Intent-based** orchestration using **standard models** (vendor cites examples such as **YANG**-oriented modeling) and **closed-loop** control for lifecycle automation. |
| **Service Activation+** | Automate **activation** of physical/virtual equipment; **templates** and **adapters** to shorten time-to-market. |
| **Service Quality Management+** | **KPIs**, quality objectives, **forecasts**—service-level visibility. |
| **Service Problem Management+** | Correlate **fault** and **performance** inputs toward **root cause** and **customer/service impact**. |

## Design intent (as stated)

- **Single interface** narrative between **service** layer and **BSS** for commercial operations.
- **Automation** of **multivendor** services **within and across** domains (5G, cloud, legacy coexistence themes in blogs).

## Comparison hook

Source A exposes **jobs**, **webhooks**, and **plugins** for automation **inside** one platform. AS1 markets **orchestration** and **activation** as **productized** modules with **adapter** ecosystems—typical of **telco OSS** procurement models.
