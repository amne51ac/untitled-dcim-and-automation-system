# Additional Source 1 — fulfillment, assurance, and closed-loop (advertised)

## Converged story

Public blog content argues that **fulfillment** and **assurance** are converging: **provisioning** decisions should use **live** health and performance context, not only static availability—especially in **hybrid** and **virtualized** networks where resources change frequently.

## Closed-loop automation

Marketing describes **closed-loop** flows where **assurance** data (fault, performance, predictive signals) informs **orchestration** and **optimization**—aligning with **AI/ML** themes in newer materials.

## Real-time inventory linkage

The **active inventory** concept is tied to **resource monitoring**: status from the network is meant to **inform** inventory accuracy during **provisioning** and **optimization**, reducing **manual reconciliation**.

## 5G and slicing

Public commentary references **network slicing** and **complex routing** as drivers for **deeper** integration between inventory, orchestration, and assurance—operator-scale concerns less central to generic enterprise DCIM tools.

## Limitations of this analysis

No claims are made here about **actual** closed-loop performance in a given customer deployment—only the **advertised** architectural direction.
